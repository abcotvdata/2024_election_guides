In New York, the Board of Elections must receive applications to register to vote no later than October 26. That includes applications by mail or in-person registration.

There are several ways to vote in New York State, including in-person and early voting.

Early voting begins in New York on Saturday, October 26.

Applications requesting to receive an early mail ballot must be received by the Board of Elections in your county no later than 10 days before the election.

There are several ways that voters can return their early mail ballot for the general election.

You can bring it to your County Board of Elections Office no later than November 5 by 9 p.m.

Voters can also bring it to an early voting poll site in your county between October 26 and November 3 or bring it to a poll site in your county on November 5 by 9 p.m.

Those voting by absentee ballot or military and overseas voting must adhere to specific deadlines per election.

For more information, visit the New York State Board of Elections.

New York City

The 2024 New York City Charter Revision Commission (CRC) approved the following five ballot proposals:


Topic Proposal
Public Safety The proposal would promote public input and deliberation before the City Council votes on local laws affecting public safety operations of the police, correction or fire departments.
Capital Planning The CRC has proposed an amendment to improve the city’s capital planning process by promoting transparency and ensuring the city requires more details to inform capital planning, and update capital planning deadlines.
Fiscal Responsibility The CRC has proposed an amendment to improve how the city assesses the fiscal impact of proposed local laws and address certain outdated and inefficient budget deadlines.
Minority-and Women-Owned Business Enterprises, Film Permits and Modernization of City Operations The CRC has proposed an amendment to update several charter provisions intended to improve city government operations, establish a Chief Business Diversity Officer and authorize the mayor to designate the office that issues film permits.
Clean Streets The proposal would expand and clarify the New York City Department of Sanitation’s authority to keep the city clean and require disposal of waste in containers.
